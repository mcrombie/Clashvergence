Strategic Interpretation

Faction4’s opportunist timing mattered because it converted a late-strength position into the highest treasury, which outweighed being down 2 regions and let 52 beat Faction3’s 45. The board stayed contested through the early and midgame, which meant larger territorial gains by Faction3 and Faction1 did not create separation, so map control never translated into a decisive lead. Faction4’s late surge was decisive because the ring remained open enough for a treasury-based finish, while the others failed to turn their regional gains into enough economic advantage.
The closest challenger was Faction3 (economic) at 45 treasury and 6 regions.

Victor's History

Faction4’s victory was a study in timing and realism: it did not seek to dominate the map, but to finish where the contest was decided, on treasury. While others accumulated regions, Faction4 stayed active and disciplined, holding four regions yet pressing the board with 16 total attacks and enough force to end first with 52 treasury. Faction3’s steadier growth to six regions and 45 treasury was respectable, but it never quite converted breadth into the decisive advantage Faction4 secured. In the end, the result favored the faction that understood when to act and when to let the larger claims of the others work against them.
