Strategic Interpretation

Aruangos Monarchy’s developmental forest approach won because the result was decided by treasury rather than territory, and its 971 total beat Arangoun State’s 887 even though the region gap was only one. After a contested opening and a midgame where Arangoun State was strongest, the decisive shift was economic because the treasury edge created by the mid-phase rebels became central to the finish, which meant larger regional swings did not translate into a decisive lead. Yanonev’s early and late surges, and Aruangos Monarchy’s own late regional gain, mattered only insofar as they kept the board fluid, because the final separation came from resource accumulation that the runner-up could not convert into enough income.

Victor's History

Aruangos Monarchy won not by spectacle but by disciplined conversion, opening quietly, holding 7 regions through the first phase, and then using the middle turns to build a treasury lead that proved decisive. Its Developmental Forest approach was well matched to the map and produced the strongest economic finish: 971 treasury, first place, and a stable 6 regions at the end. Arangoun State had a credible run and even led in the middle, but it could not sustain that position against a rival that managed its growth more efficiently and finished with greater reserve. On a thirty-seven-region ring, the result reads as a clear judgment in favor of steadier economic stewardship.
